Help & FAQ

Last updated: 2026-05-12

This page covers the most common questions about Skeinly. For anything not covered here, tap Settings > Contact Support in the app to email us with diagnostic context pre-filled, or write directly to skeinly.app@gmail.com.

How do I create a pattern?

Tap the + button on the Patterns tab. Give your pattern a name, set the gauge and yarn details if you have them (you can add these later), and then open the chart editor to lay out your stitch grid. The editor includes a symbol palette on the left and an undo/redo history along the top.

How do I share a pattern?

Open the pattern, tap Share in the toolbar, and choose either Share with a friend (sends an invitation link to a single recipient by email) or Publish to Discovery (makes the pattern browsable by all Skeinly users). Both options can be revoked later from the pattern's Sharing settings.

How do I track a project?

Create a project from a pattern by tapping Start a project. Open the project to access the row counter — tap + to advance, tap − to back up. You can attach notes and photos at any row. Progress saves automatically and syncs to your other devices when you're online.

What is a Suggestion (in Japanese: 提案)?

A Suggestion is a proposed change to another user's pattern. If you save a public pattern to your library and then improve it (e.g. add a row, fix a stitch count), you can submit a Suggestion back to the original author. They can review your change, leave comments, and either apply it to their pattern or close it. Think of it like a friendly "I noticed something — what do you think?" exchange between knitters.

How do I subscribe to Skeinly Pro?

Tap Settings > Subscribe to Pro to open the paywall. Pro unlocks advanced symbol packs and additional editing features. Pricing is shown on the paywall screen before you confirm purchase. The subscription auto-renews monthly or yearly depending on which plan you choose; you can cancel anytime (see below).

How do I cancel my subscription?

Tap Settings > Manage Subscription. This opens the platform store's subscription management screen (App Store on iOS, Play Store on Android) where you can change plans, pause, or cancel. Skeinly does not handle billing directly — Apple and Google process all subscription payments.

How do I delete my account?

Tap Settings > Delete Account. Confirm in the dialog that appears. Account deletion is immediate and permanent — all your patterns, projects, progress notes, comments, and shared content are removed from our servers. An active Skeinly Pro subscription is not automatically cancelled by account deletion; cancel it separately through Manage Subscription before deleting the account if you no longer want to be billed.

How do I report a bug?

In beta builds only (TestFlight / Play Internal Testing), tap Settings > Send Feedback or shake your device three times. The bug report flow opens a preview with the diagnostic context attached; tap Send report to submit. Reports become GitHub Issues on the public Skeinly repository — see the Privacy Policy for details on what is included. In production builds the bug reporter is disabled; reach out via Contact Support instead.

Why does Skeinly ask for push notification permission?

Push notifications are used only for collaboration events:

  • Someone opened a Suggestion on a pattern you own
  • Someone commented on a Suggestion you're involved in
  • A Suggestion was applied or closed

Push is never used for marketing or promotional content. You can decline the permission and Skeinly continues to work normally — you simply won't be notified when collaboration activity happens. You can change your mind anytime in Settings > Notifications.

Why are notifications in English when my phone is set to Japanese (and vice versa)?

Notification text is rendered server-side from the locale that was active on your device when you first granted notification permission. If you change your device's language afterward, existing push notifications continue to use the original locale until you toggle notifications off and on again (which re-registers your device with the current locale).

Does Skeinly work offline?

Yes. You can create patterns, edit charts, track project progress, and add notes/photos entirely offline. Data syncs automatically the next time you have an internet connection. The only features that require connectivity are Discovery (browsing other users' public patterns), Sharing (sending invitations), Suggestions (submitting to other users), and Pro subscription state checks.

How do I switch the app language?

Skeinly follows your device's system language. On iOS 13+ and Android 13+ you can also set a per-app language (Settings → Languages on iOS; Settings → Apps → Skeinly → Language on Android). Currently English and Japanese are supported.
